About Me

I am a Research Scientist at Rush University Medical Center in Chicago, IL. In this role, I am working on the investigation of musculoskeletal spine diseases through medical imaging and the development of computer vision methods for analysis. I took part in a Dual Degree program between the University of Illinois at Chicago and Politecnico di Milano, from which I obtained my Master of Science in Biomedical Engineering (December 2022). My research is focused on Deep Learning applied to Medical Imaging.

Machine Learning Projects

View Recognition of Mouse Brains

Designed a CNN for view recognition of mouse brains in Keras using state-of-the-art architecture (EfficientNet). The aim of the model consists in classifying mouse brain images coming from different sources (therefore dealing with imbalanced dataset issues) into horizontal view, coronal view and sagittal view.

Leveraged transfer learning techniques by fine-tuning the pre-trained EfficientNet model, reaching an F1-score of 0.98 on the test set.

Utilized Class Activation Maps to gain insights into the model’s decision-making classification process and identify areas of improvement.

Protein Family Prediction

Implementation of machine learning (KNN, Decision Trees, Bagging, XGBoost) and deep learning (LSTM network) classification models to predict which families different proteins belong, starting from features available in the Protein Data Bank (PDB).

Machine learning models were trained on a dataset including protein biophysical properties, while the deep learning model was trained using protein amino acids sequences.

The LSTM model performed better than the best machine learning classification model (XGBoost), with an increase of F1-score from 0.66 to 0.75 on the test set.

Life Expectancy Prediction

Applied linear regression (Vanilla, Ridge, Lasso) and ensemble methods (Bagging, Random Forest, XGBoost) to predict the life expectancy of a country given its main health indexes. Data cleaning was initially performed to deal with outliers and to apply different strategies for handling missing values.

The best ensemble model (XGBoost) allowed to reduce MSE up to 31% with respect to the best regression model (Ridge).
